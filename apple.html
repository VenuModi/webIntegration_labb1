Apple Baked Oatmeal

Wake up to this scrumptious Apple Baked Oatmeal! This tasty breakfast recipe is made with delicious, healthy ingredients and loaded with sweet apples and warm, cozy flavors. Best of all, it's so easy to make!

Prep time

30 min.

Cook time

45 min.

Serves

serves 10

Instructions

Step-1

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray. In a mixing bowl, combine the old-fashioned oats, chopped nuts (if using), baking powder, ground cinnamon, and salt. Mix these dry ingredients together.

Step-2

In another bowl, whisk together the milk, maple syrup or honey, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Stir everything together until well incorporated.

Step-3

Gently fold in the chopped apples and raisins or dried cranberries if you're using them. Make sure the apple pieces are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.Pour the oatmeal mixture into the greased baking dish. Use a spatula to spread it evenly.

Step-4

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 35-40 minutes or until the top is golden brown, and the oatmeal is set. It should no longer be jiggly in the center. Remove the baked oatmeal from the oven and allow it to cool slightly before serving.

Step-5

You can serve it warm with a drizzle of maple syrup, a dollop of yogurt, or a splash of milk, depending on your preference. Dig in and enjoy your homemade Apple Baked Oatmeal! It's a nutritious and comforting breakfast option that's perfect for a chilly morning.

Ingredients

2 cups old-fashioned oats

1/2 cup chopped nuts (such as walnuts or pecans), optional

1 teaspoon baking powder

1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

1/2 teaspoon salt

2 cups milk (dairy or non-dairy)

1/4 cup pure maple syrup or honey

2 large eggs.

2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract

2 apples, peeled, cored, and chopped (use your favorite variety)

1/2 cup raisins or dried cranberries (optional)

Butter or cooking spray for greasing the baking dish
